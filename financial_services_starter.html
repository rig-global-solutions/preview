Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Step 1: Initial Consultation & Qualification

Qualify lead based on target profile. Use the "First Call Checklist" to understand their primary needs and explain the Starter Package.

Step 2: Discovery Meeting

Conduct a detailed meeting using the "Client Discovery Questionnaire". Collect information on income, expenses, assets, liabilities, and financial goals.

Step 3: Proposal & Agreement

Send a standardized proposal outlining the scope of service. Upon acceptance, get the "Service Agreement" e-signed and collect the first month's fee.

Step 4: Welcome & Data Collection

Send the "Welcome Kit Email", which includes a checklist of required documents (payslips, bank statements, policy docs, etc.) and access to the secure client portal.

Step 5: Data Analysis & Plan Formulation

Input all collected data into our financial planning software. Analyze cash flow, net worth, and risk profile. Develop initial drafts of the financial, retirement, and insurance plans.

Step 6: Internal Review

The drafted plan must undergo a peer review by another financial planner to ensure accuracy, completeness, and adherence to company standards.

Step 7: Plan Presentation Meeting

Schedule a meeting with the client. Walk them through the "Financial Plan Presentation Deck". Clearly explain the findings, recommendations, and the action steps required.

Step 8: Implementation Support

Assist the client with the initial steps of implementation. This includes providing links to investment platforms, helping with forms for insurance, and setting up their budget template.

Step 9: Monthly Check-in

Initiate a brief email or 15-min call to review the client's budget and expense tracking for the month. Address any immediate questions.

Step 10: Quarterly Review

Conduct a 45-minute quarterly review meeting. Discuss progress towards goals, review investment performance, and make minor adjustments to the plan as needed.

Step 11: Annual Review

A comprehensive annual review of the entire financial plan. Update data, reassess goals, and rebalance the investment portfolio. This is also an opportunity to discuss upgrading the service package.

Hypothetical Case Study

CLIENT PROFILE

Priya Sharma

28-year-old Freelance UI/UX Designer

Background & Goals:

  • Fluctuating monthly income (₹80k - ₹1.2L).
  • Goals: Save for a down payment on a house (₹10L in 3 years), build a retirement fund, and optimize taxes as a freelancer.
  • Challenges: No structured savings plan, unsure about insurance needs, and unaware of her credit score.

Our Process in Action:

Onboarding: Priya provided her bank statements, ITRs, and completed our discovery questionnaire. We created her profile and set clear expectations.

Service Delivery: We analyzed her inconsistent cash flow and created a 'pay-yourself-first' system. We recommended a diversified portfolio of mutual funds (SIP) for her house goal, suggested NPS for retirement & tax savings, and identified a suitable term and health insurance plan.

Outcome: Priya now has a clear financial roadmap. She is consistently saving ~30% of her average monthly income, her insurance is in place, and she has a plan to improve her credit score from 720 to 780+ within a year. The monthly check-ins help her stay on track with her budget.
